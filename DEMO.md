# DEMO.md — 5-Minute Live Demo Script

> Two scenarios. Two problems solved. One system.
>
> **We operate as the healthcare provider. The patient is automated.**

---

## DEMO STRUCTURE

| Section | Duration | What It Proves |
|---------|----------|----------------|
| **Scenario 1** — Maria (newcomer, stomach pain) | ~2:30 | Patient frustration → feeling responded to, guided, reassured |
| **Transition** — System impact | ~0:30 | Capacity rebalancing, ER diversion in real time |
| **Scenario 2** — Robert (diabetic, no options) | ~1:30 | Provider overwhelm → calm, informed, in control |
| **Closing** — The before/after | ~0:30 | Stakeholder sees: problems solved, system aligned |

---

## KEY PRINCIPLES

- **The phone starts alone.** Nothing else on screen. The patient is the first thing judges see.
- **The provider side appears only after the AI has done its job.** This is the point — the system does the heavy lifting before a human ever touches it.
- **Capacity bars shift live.** ER goes down. Clinics go up. The system is balancing itself.
- **The patient always knows where they stand.** Wait position, why they're waiting, what's next.
- **We never type.** We click suggested questions, accept routing, review cards. The AI works. We decide.

---

## SCENARIO 1 — "Maria Gets Helped, Not Lost"

> **Pain point flipped:** Patient frustration, confusion, abandonment
> **Proof:** Immediate response, personalized guidance, clear next step

---

### SCENE 1.1 — The Phone (alone on screen)

**Screen:** Black/dark background. A single phone simulator, centered. Nothing else.

**What happens:**
1. Phone shows a text conversation. Contact name: **"BC HealthTriage AI"**
2. Maria's first message appears (auto-typed):
   > "Hi, I have had stomach pain for about 3 weeks. I moved here from the Philippines 2 months ago. I don't have a family doctor and my insurance hasn't started yet. I don't know where to go."
3. **Typing indicator** appears (3 dots pulsing) — the AI is responding.
4. AI responds immediately (no hold, no queue, no waiting):
   > "Hi Maria, I'm here to help. I'll ask you a few questions so we can find the right care for you. First — can you tell me your BC Services Card number, or if you don't have one yet, your date of birth?"
5. Maria responds:
   > "I don't have a card yet. My birthday is March 15, 1992."

**What judges see:** The system picked up instantly. No hold music. No "press 1 for English." A real conversation, immediately.

**Narrator note (what we say):** _"This is Maria. She's been in Canada two months. Stomach pain for three weeks. No doctor, no insurance, no idea where to go. She texted one number. The AI picked up immediately."_

**Duration:** ~30 seconds

---

### SCENE 1.2 — The Split (phone slides, provider appears)

**Screen transition:** The phone slides to the **left side** of the screen. The **right side** fades in showing the provider's view — but it's empty/minimal at first. Just a header: "CarePoint Provider" with a green "Connected" status dot.

**What happens on the phone (left, automated):**
The AI continues intake — questions appear one at a time, Maria responds automatically:

| AI Question | Maria's Response |
|-------------|-----------------|
| "Can you describe the pain? Is it sharp, dull, burning, or cramping?" | "It's like a dull ache, sometimes cramping after I eat." |
| "Any nausea, vomiting, fever, or blood in your stool?" | "Some nausea but no vomiting. No fever or blood." |
| "Are you taking any medications currently?" | "No medications." |
| "Do you have any known medical conditions or allergies?" | "No conditions. I'm allergic to sulfa drugs." |

**What happens on the provider side (right):**
As the AI gathers information, a **checklist** populates at the top of the provider panel, items checking off one by one with green checkmarks:

- [ ] Identity Verified → checks off after DOB confirmed
- [ ] Healthcare Status Validated → checks off (no BC card, no insurance, newcomer flag)
- [ ] Symptoms Collected → checks off after pain + nausea captured
- [ ] History Reviewed → checks off after meds/conditions/allergies gathered
- [ ] Triage Recommendation Ready → checks off last, with a brief animation

**The point:** The provider hasn't done anything yet. The AI did all of this. By the time a human touches this case, the work is done.

**What we say:** _"While the AI talks to Maria, the system is doing intake. Identity. Healthcare status. Symptoms. History. Triage. All before a provider ever sees this case."_

**Duration:** ~45 seconds

---

### SCENE 1.3 — The Card Appears (Live Call Dashboard)

**Screen transition:** The right side shifts from the empty provider view to the **Live Call Dashboard**. A single patient card animates in — it pulses with a soft glow, indicating: **"AI intake complete. Ready for review."**

**The card shows:**
- **Name:** Maria Santos
- **Age/Sex:** 34F
- **Chief Complaint:** Abdominal pain — 3 weeks, post-meal cramping, nausea
- **Triage Score:** 42 (Level 4 — Semi-Urgent)
- **Urgency indicator:** Yellow badge
- **AI Confidence:** 91%
- **Wait time:** 0:00 (just entered queue)
- **Status:** "Intake Complete — Awaiting Provider Review"

The card pulses gently. It's saying: _I'm ready. Look at me when you're ready._

**What we say:** _"Now we're on the provider side. Maria's card just appeared. The AI assessed her at triage level 4 — semi-urgent. 91% confidence. The provider hasn't opened this yet, but they already know: this isn't an emergency, and the AI has done the intake."_

**Duration:** ~20 seconds

---

### SCENE 1.4 — Provider Opens the File (not the conversation)

**What we do:** We **click** Maria's card.

**What happens:** The card does NOT open into a conversation. Instead, it opens Maria's **patient file view** — a context card showing everything the AI gathered:

**Patient File Card:**
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Name | Maria Santos |
| Age | 34 |
| Sex | Female |
| Language | Tagalog (English conversational) |
| BC Health Card | None — newcomer, insurance pending |
| Location | Victoria, BC |
| Conditions | None on record |
| Medications | None |
| Allergies | Sulfa drugs |
| Chief Complaint | Dull/cramping abdominal pain x3 weeks, worse after eating, associated nausea |
| AI Assessment | Possible functional dyspepsia or dietary adjustment issue. Low risk for acute abdomen. No red flags (no fever, no vomiting, no blood, no weight loss). |
| Triage | Level 4 — Semi-Urgent |
| Confidence | 91% |
| Recommendation | Community health centre or walk-in clinic. Patient is a newcomer without insurance — route to facility with translation services and newcomer programs. |

**Why this matters:** The provider goes into the conversation from a place of **knowledge**. They're not blindsided. They're not scrambling. They read the file, they understand the situation, and they enter the conversation calm and prepared.

**What we say:** _"The provider doesn't jump straight into the conversation. They open the file first. They see everything — who Maria is, what the AI found, what the recommendation is. They go in informed, not overwhelmed."_

**Duration:** ~20 seconds

---

### SCENE 1.5 — The Conversation (provider clicks through)

**What we do:** We click **"Start Conversation"** on the file card.

**Screen transition:** The file card slides away. The conversation view opens: chat transcript on the left, AI-suggested follow-up questions as clickable pills on the right.

**What's already in the chat:** The full AI intake transcript (read-only, greyed out slightly to indicate it already happened).

**AI-suggested question pills appear:**
- "Is the pain getting worse, better, or staying the same?"
- "Have you changed your diet since moving to Canada?"
- "Any stress or difficulty sleeping?"

**What we do:** We **click** "Have you changed your diet since moving to Canada?"

**Maria responds (auto):**
> "Yes, I've been eating very different food. More bread, more dairy. In the Philippines I mostly ate rice and fish."

**New pills appear:**
- "Have you noticed the pain is worse with specific foods?"
- "Any bloating or gas?"

**What we do:** We **click** "Any bloating or gas?"

**Maria responds (auto):**
> "Yes, actually. A lot of bloating after milk or cheese."

**AI insight pops up on the provider side:**
> "Pattern detected: Dietary change + bloating with dairy → possible lactose intolerance. Common in Southeast Asian populations (>90% prevalence). Recommend dietary counseling at community health centre."

**What we say:** _"The provider didn't type a word. They clicked two questions the AI suggested. The AI connected the dots — dietary change, dairy intolerance, Southeast Asian prevalence. The provider confirms and routes."_

**Duration:** ~30 seconds

---

### SCENE 1.6 — The Routing (Maria gets her answer)

**What we do:** We click **"Route Patient"**.

**What happens on the provider side:**
A routing card appears with:
- **Recommended:** Cool Aid Community Health Centre
  - Wait time: ~45 min
  - Services: Translation (Tagalog), newcomer health programs, no insurance required
  - Distance: 12 min drive
- **Alternative 1:** Victoria Walk-In Clinic — 30 min wait, no translation services
- **Alternative 2:** Virtual Care (Telus Health) — 15 min wait, English only
- **Map** shows Maria's location and the 3 options with pins

We click **"Confirm Routing"**.

**What happens on the phone (left side):**
Maria receives a message:
> "Maria, based on our conversation, we recommend Cool Aid Community Health Centre. They can see you in about 45 minutes, they have Tagalog translation services, and they serve newcomers without insurance. Here's the address and directions. If your symptoms get worse — severe pain, vomiting, fever, or blood — go to the nearest ER or call 911 immediately."

Below the message, a card appears on her phone:
- **Cool Aid Community Health Centre**
- Address + map link
- Estimated wait: 45 min
- "They're expecting you" confirmation

**What we say:** _"Maria now knows exactly where to go. Not the ER. Not five phone calls. One conversation, and she has a place that speaks her language, serves newcomers, and can see her today. That's the opposite of frustration."_

**Duration:** ~25 seconds

## TRANSITION — System Impact (the stakeholder moment)

**Screen transition:** Both sides merge into the **full-width Live Call Dashboard** showing facility capacity bars.

**What happens (animated over ~15 seconds):**

1. **ER capacity bars** — Royal Jubilee and Victoria General — tick **down** slightly (from 92% to 87%, from 89% to 84%). They shift from red/orange toward yellow.
2. **Community health / clinic bars** — Cool Aid, Westshore, James Bay — tick **up** (from 35% to 48%, from 22% to 31%). They shift from empty green toward healthy utilization.
3. A counter at the top animates: **"Patients Routed Away from ER: 1"** ... then **"2"** ... then **"3"** as we imply the system is working across many patients.
4. A secondary display shows: **"Without CarePoint"** — ER at 98% (red, overflowing) vs **"With CarePoint"** — ER at 84% (yellow, manageable). The contrast is the whole story.

**What we say:** _"Every patient like Maria who goes to the right place instead of the ER — that's capacity freed up. The ER drops. The clinics that were sitting empty start filling up. The system balances itself."_

**Duration:** ~30 seconds

## SCENARIO 2 — "Robert Gets Rerouted, Not Stuck"

> **Pain point flipped:** Provider overwhelm, patient stuck with no options
> **Proof:** System finds options the patient can't see, provider stays in control

---

### SCENE 2.1 — New Call, Higher Urgency

**Screen:** We're still on the Live Call Dashboard. A **new card** animates in — more urgently styled (amber/orange pulse).

**The card shows:**
- **Name:** Robert Chen
- **Age/Sex:** 58M
- **Chief Complaint:** Numbness and tingling in both feet, worsening over 2 weeks
- **Triage Score:** 55 (Level 3 — Urgent)
- **Known conditions:** Type 2 Diabetes (HbA1c 8.2), Hypertension
- **Medications:** Metformin, Lisinopril
- **AI Assessment:** Diabetic peripheral neuropathy — needs assessment but not ER-level emergency
- **The problem:** Family doctor is booked for 3 weeks. Nearest urgent care (Saanich UPCC) has a 2-hour wait. ER has a 4-hour wait.
- **Status:** "Intake Complete — Awaiting Provider Review"

**Phone side (left):** Robert's phone shows his completed AI conversation. He's worried. His last message:
> "My doctor can't see me for 3 weeks and I'm scared this is getting worse. I don't know what to do."

**What we say:** _"Robert is diabetic. Numbness in his feet. His doctor is booked for weeks. The urgent care has a 2-hour wait. The ER is 4 hours. He has no good options — or so he thinks."_

**Duration:** ~20 seconds

---

### SCENE 2.2 — Provider Reviews, Converses, Routes

**What we do:** Click Robert's card → open file → review context → click "Start Conversation".

**Quick conversation (2-3 clicks):**

We click: **"Can you feel temperature changes in your feet?"**
Robert: _"Not really, they feel cold most of the time but I can't tell if they actually are."_

We click: **"Any open sores or wounds on your feet?"**
Robert: _"No sores, but I noticed my toenails are getting discolored."_

**AI insight:**
> "Diabetic neuropathy with loss of temperature sensation and nail changes. Not emergent but needs assessment within 24-48 hours to prevent complications. Current HbA1c 8.2 suggests suboptimal glucose control contributing to progression."

**We route:** Saanich UPCC — 2 hour wait. Best available option right now. Robert is told:
> "Robert, we've booked you in at Saanich Peninsula Urgent Care. Current wait is about 2 hours. We'll keep monitoring — if a faster option opens up, we'll let you know immediately."

**Duration:** ~30 seconds

---

### SCENE 2.3 — The Wait (Robert sees why he's waiting)

**Screen transition:** Robert's phone now shows a **wait status screen** — his position in the queue at Saanich UPCC.

**Robert's phone shows:**

**Your Wait Status — Saanich Peninsula UPCC**
- **Your position:** 8 of 12
- **Estimated wait:** ~1 hr 45 min
- **Your triage level:** Level 3 (Urgent)

**Why you're waiting:**
- 1 cardiac event (Level 1) admitted ahead of you
- 2 acute injuries (Level 2) being treated
- You are next among Level 3 patients

> "Patients with immediately life-threatening conditions are seen first. You are the next priority in your triage level."

**A small link at the bottom:** "How does triage work?" — expandable explainer showing CTAS levels 1-5 and where Robert sits.

**What we say:** _"Robert can see exactly where he is. He can see why he's waiting — a cardiac case came in, two acute injuries. He's not just sitting in a void. He has information, so he has patience."_

**Duration:** ~20 seconds

---

### SCENE 2.4 — The Reroute (the system finds a better option)

**What happens:** A notification pops up on **both** screens simultaneously.

**On the provider dashboard:**
A banner slides in at the top:
> "System Alert: Westshore Urgent Care — 3 cancellations detected. Wait time dropped from 1hr 40min to 15 minutes. 1 queued patient is eligible for reroute."

Robert's card now has a **blue "Reroute Available"** badge.

**On Robert's phone:**
A notification card slides in:
> "Good news — Westshore Urgent Primary Care Centre just had cancellations. Their wait is now 15 minutes instead of your current 1 hr 45 min wait. It's a 20-minute drive from your location. Would you like to switch?"
>
> **[Accept — Switch to Westshore]** | **[Stay at Saanich]**
>
> _This offer expires in 5 minutes._

A countdown timer ticks down on the offer.

**What we do:** On the provider side, we see the reroute recommendation and **approve** it.

**Robert taps "Accept"** on his phone (automated).

**What happens:**
- Robert's card updates: **Saanich UPCC → Westshore UPCC**
- Wait time changes: **1:45 → 0:15**
- His phone shows new directions + updated ETA
- A message appears: _"You're confirmed at Westshore. Drive time is about 20 minutes. They're expecting you. Leave in the next 10 minutes to arrive right on time."_

**What we say:** _"The system saw something Robert couldn't — cancellations at another facility. 15 minutes instead of 2 hours. It didn't just find the option, it told him when to leave so he's not sitting in another waiting room."_

**Duration:** ~30 seconds

---

### SCENE 2.5 — The Dashboard Rebalances

**Screen transition:** Full-width dashboard view again.

**What animates:**
1. **Saanich UPCC** capacity ticks down (one fewer patient in queue)
2. **Westshore UPCC** ticks up slightly (absorbing Robert)
3. **ER bars** remain stable — Robert never went to the ER
4. A **"What If" overlay** appears briefly:
   - **Without CarePoint:** Robert goes to ER (4-hour wait, wrong setting, takes a bed from someone who needs it). ER goes to 96%.
   - **With CarePoint:** Robert goes to UPCC (15-min wait, right setting, right care). ER stays at 84%.
5. The counter updates: **"ER Visits Prevented Today: 4"**

**What we say:** _"Robert got faster care at the right facility. The ER never saw him — which means the ER stays available for actual emergencies. That's the value: not just better for Maria and Robert, but better for the entire system."_

**Duration:** ~20 seconds

## TECHNICAL NOTES FOR IMPLEMENTATION

### Screen States & Transitions

| State | Screen Layout | Transition Effect |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| `PHONE_ONLY` | Phone centered, dark background | Fade in from black |
| `SPLIT_VIEW` | Phone left (40%), Provider right (60%) | Phone slides left, provider fades in from right |
| `DASHBOARD_FULL` | Full-width Live Call Dashboard | Both sides merge/fade into full width |
| `PROVIDER_FILE` | Patient file card (overlay or panel) | Card slides up from bottom or fades in |
| `PROVIDER_CONVO` | Chat left, AI pills + facility right | File slides out, conversation slides in |
| `WAIT_STATUS` | Phone shows queue position | Smooth content transition on phone |
| `REROUTE_ALERT` | Notification banners on both sides simultaneously | Slide down from top (provider), slide up from bottom (phone) |
| `IMPACT_VIEW` | Full-width capacity comparison | Fade transition with animated bars |

### Animation Timing

- Message auto-type: 30ms per character
- Pause between messages: 1.5–2.5 seconds (feels human)
- Typing indicator: show for 1–2 seconds before AI response
- Checklist items: check off 0.5s after relevant info is captured
- Capacity bars: animate over 2 seconds with easing
- Reroute notification: slide in over 0.3s, countdown starts at 5:00
- Card pulse: 2s infinite cycle, soft green glow

### Interactive Moments (where we click)

1. **Scene 1.4** — Click Maria's card on dashboard
2. **Scene 1.5** — Click "Start Conversation" on file card
3. **Scene 1.5** — Click 2 AI-suggested question pills
4. **Scene 1.6** — Click "Route Patient" then "Confirm Routing"
5. **Scene 2.2** — Click Robert's card, review file, start conversation
6. **Scene 2.2** — Click 2 AI-suggested question pills, then route
7. **Scene 2.4** — Approve reroute on provider side
