# Healthcare Facilities — Types and Seed Instances

> Complete catalog of healthcare facility types in the Canadian system, plus 12 seeded Victoria-area instances for the CarePoint demo. ALL DATA IS MOCK/SYNTHETIC.

---

## Facility Types (18 Types)

### TYPE-01: Hospital — Level I Trauma Centre
- **Hours:** 24/7
- **Services:** Full emergency department, ICU, operating rooms, all medical/surgical specialties, medical imaging (X-ray, CT, MRI, ultrasound), laboratory, pharmacy, psychiatry, obstetrics, pediatrics
- **Capacity:** 300-500+ beds
- **Staffing:** Full multidisciplinary — ER physicians, surgeons, specialists on-call, RNs, RTs, lab techs, radiology techs, pharmacists, social workers
- **Who goes here:** Life-threatening emergencies, major trauma, complex surgical cases, high-risk obstetrics, stroke/MI requiring intervention
- **Typical wait (ER):** 2-6+ hours for non-urgent

### TYPE-02: Hospital — Community
- **Hours:** 24/7
- **Services:** Emergency department, basic surgery, medical imaging (X-ray, CT, limited MRI), laboratory, pharmacy, general medicine, some specialist clinics
- **Capacity:** 50-150 beds
- **Staffing:** ER physicians, GPs with hospital privileges, limited specialist coverage, RNs, basic lab/imaging
- **Who goes here:** Emergencies within capability, medical admissions, basic surgical cases. Transfers complex cases to Level I.
- **Typical wait (ER):** 1-4 hours

### TYPE-03: Urgent Care Centre
- **Hours:** 8am-10pm, 7 days/week (typical)
- **Services:** Walk-in acute care, X-ray, sutures, simple fracture management, wound care, abscess drainage, IV fluids, nebulizer treatments
- **Capacity:** 15-25 patients/hour
- **Staffing:** Physicians or NPs, RNs, X-ray tech
- **Who goes here:** Injuries needing X-ray/sutures, moderate infections, asthma exacerbation, dehydration, sprains/strains
- **Typical wait:** 30-90 minutes

### TYPE-04: Walk-in Clinic
- **Hours:** Weekday 8am-8pm, weekends 10am-4pm (varies)
- **Services:** Basic assessment, prescriptions, referrals, sick notes, simple lab requisitions, vaccinations
- **Capacity:** 8-12 patients/hour
- **Staffing:** 1-3 physicians or NPs, clinic staff
- **Who goes here:** Cold/flu, UTI, rash, ear infection, medication refill, one-off health issues
- **Typical wait:** 30-90 minutes

### TYPE-05: Family Practice Clinic
- **Hours:** Weekday 8am-5pm (by appointment)
- **Services:** Longitudinal primary care, chronic disease management, preventive care, mental health support, referrals, minor procedures, well-baby/prenatal
- **Capacity:** Patient panel of 1,000-2,000 per provider
- **Staffing:** Family physicians and/or NPs, clinic nurses, admin staff
- **Who goes here:** Patients with a family doctor — routine care, chronic conditions, follow-ups
- **Typical wait:** By appointment (days to weeks)

### TYPE-06: Community Health Centre
- **Hours:** Extended hours, walk-in + appointment
- **Services:** Primary care, social services, translation/interpretation, newcomer support, Indigenous health, mental health, addictions support, SDOH screening, nutrition counseling
- **Capacity:** Varies
- **Staffing:** NPs, RNs, social workers, counselors, community health workers, patient navigators
- **Who goes here:** Uninsured, newcomers, Indigenous patients, homeless, underserved populations, anyone needing wrap-around support
- **Typical wait:** 30-90 minutes (walk-in), same-day or next-day (appointment)

### TYPE-07: Pharmacy
- **Hours:** 8am-10pm daily (varies, some 24/7)
- **Services:** Medication dispensing, OTC advice, vaccination, minor ailment assessment (in some provinces — BC, AB, SK, NS), medication reviews, blood pressure checks
- **Capacity:** Continuous (no appointment needed)
- **Staffing:** Pharmacists, pharmacy technicians
- **Who goes here:** Medication pickup, OTC advice, vaccinations, minor ailment assessment, medication questions
- **Typical wait:** 5-15 minutes

### TYPE-08: Telehealth / Virtual Care
- **Hours:** 24/7 or extended hours
- **Services:** Phone/video consultation, prescription renewal, triage advice, mental health counseling, chronic disease check-in, post-operative follow-up
- **Capacity:** Scalable (multiple providers online)
- **Staffing:** Physicians, NPs, registered nurses, counselors
- **Who goes here:** Anyone with phone/internet access needing non-physical assessment, follow-ups, mental health support, prescription renewals
- **Typical wait:** 10-30 minutes

### TYPE-09: Mental Health Crisis Centre
- **Hours:** 24/7
- **Services:** Crisis stabilization, short-term counseling, psychiatric assessment, safety planning, substance use crisis support, referral to ongoing services
- **Capacity:** 10-20 clients at a time
- **Staffing:** Psychiatrists (on-call), psychiatric nurses, crisis counselors, social workers, peer support workers
- **Who goes here:** Mental health crisis (suicidal ideation, panic attacks, psychotic episodes, substance use crisis) as an alternative to ER
- **Typical wait:** 15-45 minutes

### TYPE-10: Home Care Nursing
- **Hours:** By referral, scheduled visits (typically daytime, some evening/weekend)
- **Services:** Wound care, medication management, vital sign monitoring, post-discharge support, palliative care, chronic disease monitoring, personal care
- **Capacity:** Caseload-based (10-15 patients per nurse per day)
- **Staffing:** RNs, LPNs, home care aides, occupational therapists, physiotherapists
- **Who goes here:** Post-surgical patients, seniors with complex needs, palliative patients, anyone needing skilled nursing at home
- **Typical wait:** Referral-based (1-7 days for initial visit)

### TYPE-11: Dental Clinic
- **Hours:** Weekday 8am-5pm (by appointment)
- **Services:** Emergency dental (pain, broken tooth, abscess), routine care, fillings, extractions, oral surgery, dental hygiene
- **Capacity:** 4-8 patients per dentist per day
- **Staffing:** Dentists, dental hygienists, dental assistants
- **Who goes here:** Dental pain/emergency, routine dental care
- **Typical wait:** Same-day for emergency, days-weeks for routine

### TYPE-12: Physiotherapy Clinic
- **Hours:** Weekday 7am-7pm (by appointment)
- **Services:** Rehabilitation, injury recovery, chronic pain management, post-surgical rehab, workplace injury, sports injury, mobility assessment
- **Capacity:** 1 therapist sees 8-12 patients per day
- **Staffing:** Physiotherapists, physiotherapy assistants, kinesiologists
- **Who goes here:** Post-injury/surgery rehabilitation, chronic pain, mobility issues
- **Typical wait:** By appointment (days to weeks)

### TYPE-13: Optometry Clinic
- **Hours:** Weekday 9am-5pm (by appointment)
- **Services:** Eye exams, vision correction prescriptions, urgent eye issues (foreign body, sudden vision change), glaucoma monitoring, diabetic retinopathy screening
- **Capacity:** 15-20 patients per day
- **Staffing:** Optometrists, optometric assistants
- **Who goes here:** Routine eye care, sudden vision changes, eye injuries
- **Typical wait:** By appointment (days), same-day for urgent

### TYPE-14: Drug Store / Retail Pharmacy
- **Hours:** Extended hours (7am-11pm, some 24/7)
- **Services:** OTC medication, health products, basic health screening (BP machines), first aid supplies
- **Capacity:** Continuous
- **Staffing:** Retail staff (no clinical staff on-site)
- **Who goes here:** OTC medication purchase, health products, convenience
- **Typical wait:** None

### TYPE-15: Long-term Care Facility
- **Hours:** Residential, 24/7
- **Services:** Nursing care, personal care, medication management, social activities, physiotherapy, dietary management, palliative care
- **Capacity:** 50-200 beds
- **Staffing:** RNs (charge), LPNs, care aides, recreation therapists, dietary staff, visiting physicians
- **Who goes here:** Elderly with complex chronic needs requiring 24/7 nursing, dementia patients
- **Typical wait:** Waitlist-based (months to years in many provinces)

### TYPE-16: Public Health Unit
- **Hours:** Weekday 9am-5pm (appointment + walk-in)
- **Services:** Immunization, STI testing/treatment, prenatal/postnatal care, health promotion, disease surveillance, travel health, smoking cessation
- **Capacity:** Varies by service
- **Staffing:** Public health nurses, health inspectors, health promoters
- **Who goes here:** Vaccinations, sexual health, prenatal care, public health concerns
- **Typical wait:** 15-30 minutes (walk-in), by appointment for scheduled

### TYPE-17: Indigenous Health Centre
- **Hours:** Weekday 9am-5pm (appointment + walk-in)
- **Services:** Culturally safe primary care, traditional healing integration, NIHB (Non-Insured Health Benefits) navigation, mental health and addictions support, elder care, community health
- **Capacity:** Varies
- **Staffing:** Physicians/NPs, Indigenous health workers, elders, traditional healers, social workers, NIHB navigators
- **Who goes here:** Indigenous patients seeking culturally safe care, anyone needing NIHB navigation
- **Typical wait:** 15-45 minutes

### TYPE-18: Addictions Treatment Centre
- **Hours:** Varies (some 24/7 for detox, daytime for outpatient)
- **Services:** Detoxification, counseling, medication-assisted treatment (methadone, suboxone), harm reduction, group therapy, residential treatment
- **Capacity:** 10-50 beds (residential), 20-40 clients/day (outpatient)
- **Staffing:** Addictions counselors, physicians, psychiatric nurses, social workers, peer support
- **Who goes here:** Substance use disorder treatment, detox, harm reduction
- **Typical wait:** Varies widely (days for crisis, weeks-months for residential)
