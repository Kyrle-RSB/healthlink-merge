# Hospital Staff — Roles Reference

> Complete catalog of healthcare staff roles found in hospital and community care settings. Each role includes scope, department, client-facing status, skills, and shift patterns. ALL DATA IS MOCK/SYNTHETIC.

---

## Staff Roles (22 Roles)

### STAFF-ROLE-01: Emergency Physician
- **Department:** Emergency
- **Client-Facing:** Yes
- **Scope:** Diagnose and treat patients presenting to the ER. Make admit/discharge/transfer decisions. Order investigations (labs, imaging). Perform emergency procedures. Coordinate with specialists for consults.
- **Skills:** Emergency medicine, trauma assessment, ACLS, PALS, procedural sedation, fracture reduction, laceration repair, intubation, central lines
- **Shift Pattern:** 12-hour rotating (7am-7pm, 7pm-7am), typically 14-16 shifts/month
- **Typical per facility:** 2-4 on duty per shift (large hospital), 1-2 (community hospital)

### STAFF-ROLE-02: Family Physician / General Practitioner
- **Department:** Primary Care / Family Practice Clinic
- **Client-Facing:** Yes
- **Scope:** Longitudinal primary care. Chronic disease management. Preventive care. Referrals to specialists. Prescription management. Minor procedures. Mental health support.
- **Skills:** Comprehensive primary care, chronic disease management, minor procedures, preventive medicine, mental health assessment, obstetric care (some)
- **Shift Pattern:** Weekday 8am-5pm (clinic hours), some evenings/weekends at walk-in clinics
- **Typical per facility:** 1-6 per clinic; solo or group practice

### STAFF-ROLE-03: Specialist Physician
- **Department:** Varies (Cardiology, Orthopedics, Gastroenterology, Neurology, Psychiatry, Obstetrics, Ophthalmology, etc.)
- **Client-Facing:** Yes
- **Scope:** Focused diagnosis and treatment within specialty area. Consultations from ER and primary care. Procedures specific to specialty. Follow-up for complex conditions.
- **Skills:** Specialty-specific (e.g., cardiac catheterization, joint replacement, endoscopy, EEG interpretation)
- **Shift Pattern:** Weekday 8am-4pm clinic + on-call rotation for hospital consults
- **Typical per facility:** 1-3 per specialty at community hospitals; 5-20+ at academic/tertiary centers

### STAFF-ROLE-04: Nurse Practitioner (NP)
- **Department:** Primary Care, Urgent Care, Community Health
- **Client-Facing:** Yes
- **Scope:** Independent patient assessment, diagnosis, prescribing. Manages own patient panel. Orders and interprets investigations. Manages chronic diseases. Referrals. Works autonomously within legislated scope.
- **Skills:** Advanced health assessment, independent prescribing, chronic disease management, minor procedures, health promotion, patient education
- **Shift Pattern:** Weekday 8am-6pm (clinic), some extended hours at urgent care
- **Typical per facility:** 1-3 per clinic/urgent care center

### STAFF-ROLE-05: Registered Nurse (RN)
- **Department:** All clinical departments (ER, ICU, Med-Surg, OR, Obstetrics, Psychiatry, Community)
- **Client-Facing:** Yes
- **Scope:** Patient assessment. Medication administration. IV therapy. Wound care. Patient education. Care coordination. Documentation (600-800 data points per 12h shift). Monitoring vital signs. Communicating with physicians and families.
- **Skills:** Patient assessment, medication administration, IV insertion, wound care, catheterization, patient education, crisis response, documentation
- **Shift Pattern:** 12-hour rotating (7am-7pm, 7pm-7am), some 8-hour shifts in clinic settings
- **Typical per facility:** Largest staff group — 1 RN per 4-6 patients (med-surg), 1:1-2 (ICU)

### STAFF-ROLE-06: Licensed Practical Nurse (LPN)
- **Department:** Med-Surg, Long-term Care, Clinics
- **Client-Facing:** Yes
- **Scope:** Basic nursing care under RN supervision. Vital signs. Medication administration (within scope). Personal care. Wound care (basic). Documentation. Patient transport assistance.
- **Skills:** Vital signs, medication administration, basic wound care, personal care, documentation, patient mobility assistance
- **Shift Pattern:** 8-hour or 12-hour shifts, rotating
- **Typical per facility:** Complements RN staffing, often 1 LPN per 6-8 patients

### STAFF-ROLE-07: Triage Nurse
- **Department:** Emergency
- **Client-Facing:** Yes
- **Scope:** First clinical contact in ER. Performs initial assessment using CTAS (Canadian Triage and Acuity Scale). Assigns urgency level (1-5). Initiates basic interventions. Manages patient flow and queue. Reassesses waiting patients.
- **Skills:** CTAS assessment, rapid clinical assessment, crisis communication, de-escalation, vital signs, pain assessment, priority setting
- **Shift Pattern:** 12-hour rotating, aligned with ER shifts
- **Typical per facility:** 1-2 per ER at any time

### STAFF-ROLE-08: Charge Nurse
- **Department:** All clinical units
- **Client-Facing:** Partially (coordinates rather than provides direct bedside care)
- **Scope:** Unit leadership during shift. Staff assignments. Patient flow management. Resource allocation. Conflict resolution. Communication with bed management and administration. Escalation point for clinical issues.
- **Skills:** Leadership, resource management, conflict resolution, clinical expertise, communication, crisis management
- **Shift Pattern:** 12-hour rotating, one per unit per shift
- **Typical per facility:** 1 per clinical unit per shift

### STAFF-ROLE-09: Paramedic / EMT
- **Department:** Pre-hospital / Emergency Medical Services
- **Client-Facing:** Yes
- **Scope:** Emergency response and assessment in the field. Patient stabilization. Transport to appropriate facility. Advanced life support (paramedics). Basic life support (EMTs). Handoff to ER team.
- **Skills:** ACLS/BLS, trauma assessment, airway management, IV/IO access, medication administration, extrication, patient transport
- **Shift Pattern:** 12-hour rotating (24/7 coverage)
- **Typical per facility:** Based at EMS stations, not hospital-based (but interact at ER)

### STAFF-ROLE-10: Pharmacist
- **Department:** Pharmacy (hospital and community)
- **Client-Facing:** Yes
- **Scope:** Medication dispensing and verification. Drug interaction checking. Patient counseling on medication use. Vaccination (community). Minor ailment assessment (in some provinces). Medication reconciliation on admission/discharge.
- **Skills:** Pharmacology, drug interaction assessment, patient counseling, medication reconciliation, vaccination, compounding
- **Shift Pattern:** Weekday 8am-6pm (hospital), extended hours (community pharmacy), some 24/7 hospital coverage
- **Typical per facility:** 2-5 per hospital; 1-2 per community pharmacy

### STAFF-ROLE-11: Respiratory Therapist (RT)
- **Department:** ER, ICU, Med-Surg, Respiratory
- **Client-Facing:** Yes
- **Scope:** Ventilator management. Oxygen therapy. Breathing treatments (nebulizers, inhalers). Arterial blood gas sampling. Airway management assistance. Patient education on respiratory devices. Sleep studies.
- **Skills:** Ventilator management, oxygen therapy, nebulizer treatment, ABG sampling, intubation assistance, CPAP/BiPAP management, pulmonary function testing
- **Shift Pattern:** 12-hour rotating (24/7 coverage in hospitals with ICU)
- **Typical per facility:** 2-4 per hospital

### STAFF-ROLE-12: Laboratory Technologist
- **Department:** Laboratory / Pathology
- **Client-Facing:** No (minimal patient contact for blood draws)
- **Scope:** Analyze blood, urine, tissue samples. Operate laboratory equipment. Report results to ordering physicians. Quality control. Urgently flag critical values.
- **Skills:** Hematology, chemistry, microbiology, urinalysis, blood bank, quality assurance, equipment operation
- **Shift Pattern:** 8-hour rotating (24/7 coverage)
- **Typical per facility:** 5-15 per hospital lab

### STAFF-ROLE-13: Radiology Technologist
- **Department:** Medical Imaging
- **Client-Facing:** Partially (positions patients, operates equipment)
- **Scope:** Operate imaging equipment (X-ray, CT, MRI, ultrasound). Position patients. Ensure image quality. Radiation safety. Transmit images to radiologists for interpretation.
- **Skills:** X-ray operation, CT operation, MRI operation, patient positioning, radiation safety, image quality assessment
- **Shift Pattern:** 8-hour rotating (24/7 coverage for ER X-ray/CT)
- **Typical per facility:** 5-10 per hospital

### STAFF-ROLE-14: Social Worker
- **Department:** All departments / Discharge Planning
- **Client-Facing:** Yes
- **Scope:** Discharge planning. Community resource connection. Crisis support. Counseling. Financial assistance navigation. Elder abuse assessment. Child protection. Substance abuse referral. Housing support.
- **Skills:** Crisis intervention, counseling, community resource knowledge, discharge planning, advocacy, cultural competency, conflict resolution
- **Shift Pattern:** Weekday 8am-5pm (hospital), some on-call coverage
- **Typical per facility:** 2-6 per hospital

### STAFF-ROLE-15: Patient Navigator
- **Department:** Administration / Clinical Support
- **Client-Facing:** Yes
- **Scope:** Help patients navigate the healthcare system. Book appointments. Coordinate between providers. Follow up on referrals. Explain processes. Connect to community resources. Translate system complexity into plain language.
- **Skills:** System navigation, appointment scheduling, care coordination, patient communication, resource knowledge, cultural competency, multilingual (often)
- **Shift Pattern:** Weekday 8am-5pm
- **Typical per facility:** 1-3 per hospital or community health centre

### STAFF-ROLE-16: Unit Clerk / Ward Clerk
- **Department:** All clinical units
- **Client-Facing:** Partially (phone, desk interactions)
- **Scope:** Order entry in hospital system. Phone management for the unit. Scheduling. Chart management. Patient registration at unit level. Communication hub between staff, patients, and other departments.
- **Skills:** Hospital information system operation, phone management, scheduling, medical terminology, multitasking, communication
- **Shift Pattern:** 8-hour shifts (typically day and evening)
- **Typical per facility:** 1 per clinical unit per shift

### STAFF-ROLE-17: Admitting / Registration Clerk
- **Department:** Admitting / Patient Registration
- **Client-Facing:** Yes
- **Scope:** Patient registration. Insurance/coverage verification. Bed assignment coordination. Data entry. Patient identification. Privacy/consent forms.
- **Skills:** Data entry, insurance verification, patient communication, hospital information system, privacy compliance
- **Shift Pattern:** 8-hour rotating (24/7 in hospitals with ER)
- **Typical per facility:** 2-4 per hospital

### STAFF-ROLE-18: Health Records Technician
- **Department:** Health Information Management
- **Client-Facing:** No
- **Scope:** Chart coding (ICD-10, CCI). Record management. Data quality assurance. Release of information requests. Statistical reporting. Compliance monitoring.
- **Skills:** Medical coding (ICD-10-CA, CCI), health information systems, data quality, privacy compliance, statistical analysis
- **Shift Pattern:** Weekday 8am-4pm
- **Typical per facility:** 3-8 per hospital

### STAFF-ROLE-19: Porter / Transport Aide
- **Department:** All departments
- **Client-Facing:** Partially (transports patients)
- **Scope:** Patient transport between departments (ER to imaging, unit to OR, etc.). Equipment movement. Specimen transport. Assist with patient transfers.
- **Skills:** Patient handling, wheelchair/stretcher operation, way-finding, communication, basic safety
- **Shift Pattern:** 8-hour rotating
- **Typical per facility:** 5-15 per hospital

### STAFF-ROLE-20: Environmental Services / Housekeeping
- **Department:** All departments
- **Client-Facing:** No (works around patients)
- **Scope:** Cleaning and disinfection. Room turnover after discharge. Infection control cleaning protocols. Waste management. Linen management.
- **Skills:** Infection control protocols, cleaning procedures, WHMIS, waste handling, time management
- **Shift Pattern:** 8-hour rotating (24/7 coverage)
- **Typical per facility:** 15-40 per hospital

### STAFF-ROLE-21: Psychiatrist
- **Department:** Mental Health / Psychiatry
- **Client-Facing:** Yes
- **Scope:** Psychiatric assessment and diagnosis. Medication management for mental health conditions. Crisis intervention. Involuntary admission assessment (Form 1 in Ontario). Therapy. Consultation to ER and other departments.
- **Skills:** Psychiatric assessment, psychopharmacology, crisis intervention, psychotherapy, capacity assessment, risk assessment (suicide, violence)
- **Shift Pattern:** Weekday 8am-5pm (clinic) + on-call rotation for ER consults and involuntary assessments
- **Typical per facility:** 1-5 per hospital; long waitlists for outpatient

### STAFF-ROLE-22: Community Health Worker
- **Department:** Community Health / Outreach
- **Client-Facing:** Yes
- **Scope:** Home visits. Health education. Cultural liaison. Community outreach. Patient navigation for underserved populations. Connect patients to social services. Preventive health. Chronic disease self-management support.
- **Skills:** Cultural competency, health education, community resource knowledge, patient advocacy, multilingual (often), motivational interviewing
- **Shift Pattern:** Weekday 9am-5pm (flexible for community needs)
- **Typical per facility:** 1-4 per community health centre
